Thomas Glenn

Web Developer

About Thomas: A citizen of both the US and Canada, Thomas has spent his entire life pursuing a career in classical music. Wanting to avoid so much travel and to be closer to his family, Thomas decided to make a career change and is now training for a career in tech. Currently, he is a student at the Epicodus school in Portland, Oregon. He enjoys the pairs programming format because it forces him to describe and create his code in the presence of others, and learn shortcuts and different points of view.

Thomas is married (as of 23 years) with four children ages 20, 20, 10 and 4. He is also a volunteer community basketball coach, and advisor to the youth at his local church.

Thomas has had a great time creating webpages thus far at Epicodus. Here are a list of his assigned projects to date:

  • Animal Shelter is a project about a fictional animal shelter which features eight animals that need homes. Rescue your favorite and bring him/her home! This site uses .html .css floats and columns.
  • Dream Vacation highlights three outriggers giving tours to the Great Barrier Reef. This is four sites in one, using .html, .css, and bootstrap 3.3.7.
  • Cupcake Shop features a fictional cupcake store in the Western US, with links and photos in .html and .css.
  • Band Project is a page highlighting my favorite techno/disco dance band, Jamiroquai. This was my first attempt at frames, margins, and columns using .html and .css.
  • Audrey the Climber highlights my daughter and her fledgling climbing career. She is an avid rock climbing instructor and gym enthusiast.
  • Cookie Recipe was an assignment in .html to add inline and block style elements to a recipe cloned from a third-party GitHub repository.
  • Dog Blog features our new dog in one of my first .html pages and my first attempt at background color.. OH YEAH!!!
  • Hello World is my first assignment with epicodus. .html only.
  • Resort is a site featuring a fictional resort in Costa Rica called "Tres Bananas." It uses links to other sites and a few photos, using .html and .css.
